# History of the Kingdom of France

---
*481*
### Clovis Becomes King
Clovis I unites most of Gaul under the Frankish rule.
- Begins the Merovingian dynasty
- Converts to Christianity
- First king to unite all Frankish tribes[web:1]

---
*843*
### Treaty of Verdun
The Carolingian Empire splits.
- Charles the Bald receives West Francia
- Foundation for the medieval Kingdom of France[web:3][web:5][web:7]

---
*987*
### Capetian Dynasty Begins
Hugh Capet crowned King of the Franks.
- End of the Carolingian line
- Start of stable, hereditary monarchy[web:1][web:3][web:5]

---
*1190*
### King of France Title
Philip II is the first to call himself “King of France.”
> Marked a shift to a more clearly French national identity.[web:3]

---
*1337–1453*
### Hundred Years' War
Continuous conflict with England over the French throne.
1. Led to rise of Joan of Arc
2. Weakened feudal nobles
3. Ended with French victory and lost English territories[web:1][web:3]

---
*1598*
### Edict of Nantes
Henry IV grants religious freedom to Huguenots.
- Ends the French Wars of Religion
- Promotes internal peace[web:1]

---
*1643–1715*
### The Reign of Louis XIV
The “Sun King” rules France at its height.
- Absolute monarchy strengthens
- Builds the Palace of Versailles
- France becomes Europe’s dominant power[web:1][web:3]

---
*1789*
### French Revolution Begins
Revolutionaries overthrow the monarchy.
- End of the Bourbon monarchy (temporarily)
- Declaration of the Rights of Man and Citizen[web:1][web:3]

---
*1792*
### Abolition of the Monarchy
France becomes a republic.
> The Kingdom of France is officially dissolved.[web:3]
